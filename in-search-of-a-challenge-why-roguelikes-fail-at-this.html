In search of a novel challenge: Why Roguelikes fail at this.

September 2, 2024 Day

I'm playing 'tales and tactics' and after a few hours I've gotten the hang of the auto chess mechanics and the game is unlocking more starting perks so the game is getting literally easier. Then I notice that there are diffcultly levels you can climb to make the game challenging again. I do the first one, and while it adds unique challenges to the game it still feels 90% the same game. Then I quit the game and consider it done.

Why should I bother with optional challenges when I've seen 90% of the game already. Upping the diffcultly just means I'll be forced to find the 'winning strategies' and just stick to those which sounds really boring. In the case of 'Tales and Tactics' certain starting characters seem way better than others. So why shouldn't I just try to do my 'winning strategy' every time. The only reward you get for beating the higher diffculty is more diffcultly levels which is a poor reward for someone who wants to beat the game. It is a treadmill, and honestly I like running, but I also love variety. So without a new carrot to tease me to reach higher I'll just play something else that will offer a novel challenge.

Hades is maybe the only roguelike that manages to entice me to play at a higher diffculty.
